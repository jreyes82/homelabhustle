How I Got My CompTIA A+ for Free Through Calbright College

Career March 2026 6 min read

When I started looking into getting my CompTIA A+, the first thing I did was buy a Udemy course. It was fine — good content, self-paced, covered the material well. But then I started doing the math on the actual exams. The A+ certification requires passing two separate exams, Core 1 (220-1101) and Core 2 (220-1102), and each one costs around $250. That's $500 out of pocket just to sit the tests, on top of whatever you spent on study materials.

I started looking for alternatives and found Calbright College.

What is Calbright College

Calbright is a fully online California community college built specifically for working adults. No physical campus, no set class times — everything is self-paced and done online. And because it's a California community college, it's free for California residents.

The part that got my attention was their IT Support program. It's designed to prepare you for the CompTIA A+ certification, and it covers both Core 1 and Core 2. But more importantly — Calbright covers the cost of the certification exams. Both of them. That's $500 in exam vouchers at no cost to you.

How the Program Works

The curriculum is split into two courses — IT501 covering Core 1 material and IT502 covering Core 2. Both are fully online and self-paced, which means you move through the material at whatever speed works for you. There are no live lectures you have to attend at a specific time.

The content quality was on par with what I had on Udemy — same topics, same depth. What Calbright adds that Udemy doesn't is actual support:

  • One-on-one tutoring sessions you can book whenever you need help
  • Group tutoring sessions where you work through material with other students
  • A success coach who checks in on your progress

The tutoring was genuinely useful. When I was working through subnetting or a particularly dry section on hardware, being able to book a session with someone who could explain it differently made a real difference.

The Timeline

From enrolling to having the A+ cert in hand took me less than six months. I was working through the material alongside my job, so I wasn't studying full time. The self-paced structure helped — some weeks I covered a lot of ground, other weeks almost nothing, and that was fine.

Note: Calbright moves at your pace but they do have advisors who follow up if you go quiet for too long. That light accountability actually helped me stay on track.

The Exam Process

Once you complete the coursework, Calbright provides the exam vouchers. You schedule the exams through Pearson VUE — you can take them at a testing center or remotely with a live proctor. I'd recommend reading up on the remote proctoring requirements before exam day — there are specific rules about your environment, what's on your desk, and what you can have in the room.

Core 1 covers hardware, networking, mobile devices, and virtualization. Core 2 covers operating systems, security, troubleshooting, and operational procedures. Both use a mix of multiple choice and performance-based questions where you actually perform tasks in a simulated environment.

Is It Worth It vs Udemy

The study content is roughly equivalent. If you just need the material, Udemy gets you there. But Calbright gives you the exam vouchers, the tutoring support, and an actual college credential on top of the certification — all for free. For anyone in California who's serious about getting the A+, it's a straightforward decision.

I'm currently working through the Network+ program at Calbright using the same approach. Same free model, same support structure, same exam vouchers at the end. If you're in California and working toward any CompTIA cert, it's worth looking into before you pay out of pocket.

How to Enroll

  • Go to calbright.org
  • You must be a California resident
  • You must have a high school diploma or GED
  • Enrollment is open and rolling — no semester start dates
  • The application takes about 10 minutes
