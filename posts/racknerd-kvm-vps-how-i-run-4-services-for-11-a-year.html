Why KVM Matters

When shopping for cheap VPS hosting you'll see two types — OpenVZ and KVM. OpenVZ is container-based virtualization where resources are shared at the OS level. KVM is full hardware virtualization — your VPS behaves like a real dedicated machine with its own kernel.

For a homelab use case, KVM is worth the slight premium because:

  • You can run Docker without workarounds
  • Custom kernel modules work
  • Performance is more predictable
  • You're not affected by noisy neighbors at the kernel level

RackNerd's cheap deals are all KVM, which is part of why they're such good value.
